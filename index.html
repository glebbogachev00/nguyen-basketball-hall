Kobe 6
Nike · 2010
Low-Top Flywire Zoom Air Lightweight

Kobe 5
Nike · 2009
Low-Top Zoom Air Carbon Fiber Snake Scale

The shoe that changed basketball footwear forever. The Kobe 5 was the first signature shoe to prove a low-top could protect and perform at the highest level. FAMOUS COURTS The world's most iconic basketball arenas
Madison Square Garden
New York, NY NBA · Knicks Cap. 19,812 Est. 1968

Known as "The World's Most Famous Arena," MSG has hosted some of the greatest moments in basketball history. Home to the New York Knicks since 1968, it sits in the heart of Midtown Manhattan and has seen legends like Willis Reed, Patrick Ewing, and Walt Frazier define an era. Its electric atmosphere makes it one of the most intimidating venues to play in the entire NBA.

TD Garden
Boston, MA NBA · Celtics Cap. 19,156 Est. 1995

Home of the Boston Celtics — the most decorated franchise in NBA history with 17 championships — TD Garden carries an unmatched winning tradition. Opened in 1995 on the site of the old Boston Garden, it preserves the legacy of Bill Russell, Larry Bird, and Paul Pierce. The parquet floor is a symbol of Celtic pride, and the crowd is renowned as the loudest and most passionate in the league.

Crypto.com Arena
Los Angeles, CA NBA · Lakers · Clippers Cap. 19,079 Est. 1999

Crypto.com Arena — formerly the Staples Center — is the crown jewel of Los Angeles sports. Shared by the Lakers and Clippers, it has witnessed Kobe Bryant's 81-point game, four Laker championships, and some of the most memorable playoff runs in NBA history. Situated in downtown LA, it opened in 1999 and quickly became one of the most glamorous and star-studded arenas in professional sports.

United Center
Chicago, IL NBA · Bulls Cap. 20,917 Est. 1994

The United Center — nicknamed "The House That Michael Built" — is where Jordan and the Bulls dynasty cemented their legacy with six NBA championships across the 1990s. Opened in 1994, it is the largest arena in the NBA by capacity and stands adjacent to a famous bronze statue of Jordan. It remains one of the most iconic venues in sports, a living monument to the greatest era of Chicago basketball.
